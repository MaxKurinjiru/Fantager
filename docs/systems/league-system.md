# League System

Reference: [game-summary.md](../game-summary.md#212-league-system)

Purpose: Document season scheduling, promotion/relegation, fixtures, and rewards.

## League Structure Configuration

The league structure is configured per Kingdom via `league_tiers_config` (JSON field on the `Kingdom` entity). The total player capacity of a Kingdom is fully derived from this config — there is no separate `max_players` field.

### Default configuration (60-player kingdom)

```json
{
  "teams_per_group": 10,
  "tiers": [
    {
      "name": "T1",
      "groups": ["1-A"],
      "promotion_slots": 0,
      "relegation_slots": 4
    },
    {
      "name": "T2",
      "groups": ["2-A", "2-B"],
      "promotion_slots": 4,
      "relegation_slots": 6
    },
    {
      "name": "T3",
      "groups": ["3-A", "3-B", "3-C"],
      "promotion_slots": 6,
      "relegation_slots": 0
    }
  ]
}
```

| Tier | Groups | Teams | Promotion Slots (Tier total / per Group) | Relegation Slots (Tier total / per Group) |
|------|--------|-------|------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| T1   | 1      | 10    | 0 / 0                                    | 4 / 4                                     |
| T2   | 2      | 20    | 4 / 2                                    | 6 / 3                                     |
| T3   | 3      | 30    | 6 / 2                                    | 0 / 0                                     |
| **Total** | **6** | **60** | **10**                                   | **10**                                    |

Capacity formula: `sum(tier.groups) × teams_per_group` = (1 + 2 + 3) × 10 = **60**.
*(Note: If `groups` is an array of custom names, the group count is the number of elements in the array.)*

The config can be changed per Kingdom at creation time. The number of tiers, groups per tier, and teams per group are all adjustable. The Kingdom initialization step reads `league_tiers_config` to create the correct number of NPC teams, tiers, and groups.

When a new Kingdom is created, the league is set up automatically as part of Kingdom initialization (see [kingdom-system.md](kingdom-system.md#kingdom-initialization)):

1. **`LeagueSeason`** (season 1) is created (`status = scheduled` or `active` based on start date).
2. **`LeagueTier` and `LeagueGroup` rows** are created from `league_tiers_config` — the bracket skeleton exists but is empty.
3. **NPC teams are created and immediately placed** into groups — each new NPC team gets a `LeagueStanding` row in its assigned group in the same step. All groups are filled before the Kingdom opens.
4. **Heroes are generated** and assigned to each NPC team — **10 heroes per team** so every team is fully staffed from day one.
5. **Fixtures are scheduled** according to the Kingdom's game speed and season length.

When a real player takes over an NPC team, they inherit that team's existing league standing and fixtures. There is no mid-season re-seeding.

## Season Lifecycle and Scheduling

A league season is kingdom-specific and follows the timeline defined in [Calendar & Server Ticks System](calendar-system.md).

- **Season Length:** Extended to **77 days (11 weeks)** to accommodate the 1-week preparation gap, the 18 double round-robin matches, and the post-season transition phase.
- **Weekly Schedule:**
  - **Week 1:** Preparation Gap. No league matches are scheduled.
  - **Weeks 2 to 10:** 2 rounds per week:
    - **Tuesday at 18:00:** Odd-numbered rounds (Rounds 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15, 17).
    - **Friday at 18:00:** Even-numbered rounds (Rounds 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18).
- **Post-Season Transition:** Occurs on the last Friday of the season (Day 74) at 19:00, resolving promotions, relegations, rewards distribution, and generating the fixtures for the next season.

## Match Scheduling and Processing

### Pairing Algorithm (Berger's Algorithm)
To generate the round-robin fixtures for a group of $N$ teams (where $N = 10$ is the default), a **double round-robin Berger's Algorithm** is used:
1. Teams are assigned a temporary number from $1$ to $N$.
2. For each round $r \in \{1, \dots, 2N-2\}$ (18 rounds total), pairings are generated by matching teams twice (once Home and once Away).
3. To guarantee a balanced home/away distribution:
   - The fixture generator must enforce that **within each 2-round week of league play (Weeks 2–10)**, every team plays **exactly 1 Home match** and **exactly 1 Away match**.
   - Mathematically, for any team $i$ and week of play $w_{play} \in \{1, \dots, 9\}$ (corresponding to calendar Weeks $2 \dots 10$), the home status $H_i$ in the two weekly rounds must satisfy:
     $$H_i(2w_{play} - 1) \neq H_i(2w_{play})$$
     where $H_i(round) = 1$ if playing Home, and $0$ if playing Away.
   - Over the entire season, each team will play exactly 9 Home and 9 Away matches.

### Match Resolution Flow
1. At the scheduled tick time (Tuesday/Friday 18:00), the scheduling service identifies all `LeagueFixture` entries with state `scheduled` and `scheduledAt <= now`.
2. For each fixture:
   - Verify team roster eligibility (at least 6 combat-ready heroes).
   - If one or both teams fail eligibility, resolve via forfeit rules (3-0 or 0-0).
   - If both are eligible, queue the match in the combat worker queue (Redis).
3. The Combat Simulation Engine resolves the battle, updates team stats (Gold, XP, morale, form, fatigue, aging), creates a `Battle` entity, and links it to the `LeagueFixture` via `LeagueFixtureCompletionService::complete()`. Lineups are resolved from fixture assignments (`NULL` → team default formation; see [formation-system.md](formation-system.md)). Temporary match-specific formations are removed on subsequent kingdom ticks (`DailyReset`, `LeagueMatch`).
4. Update `LeagueStanding` values (played, wins, draws, losses, points, goalDifference) for both teams in the group.

## Promotion/Relegation Rules

At the end of each season (during the **Season Transition** on Week 11, Friday at 19:00), teams are reassigned between tiers based on the promotion and relegation slots configured in `league_tiers_config`.

### 1. Capacity Balance Principle
To ensure that all groups remain at their configured size (default 10 teams per group), the system enforces a strict balance:
- The total number of teams relegated from Tier $T$ to Tier $T+1$ must exactly equal the number of teams promoted from Tier $T+1$ to Tier $T$.
- This means the number of relegation slots of Tier $T$ must match the promotion slots of Tier $T+1$:
  $$\text{Tier}(T).\text{relegation\_slots} = \text{Tier}(T+1).\text{promotion\_slots}$$

### 2. Selecting Teams for Promotion
Let $P$ be the number of promotion slots for Tier $T+1$, and $G$ be the number of groups in Tier $T+1$.
- **Case $P = G$ (Balanced):** The winner (1st place) of each of the $G$ groups in Tier $T+1$ is promoted.
- **Case $P < G$ (Fewer spots than groups):** The 1st place teams from all $G$ groups are compared using the **Global Standing Comparison Formula** (see below). The top $P$ teams are promoted; the remaining group winners do not promote.
- **Case $P > G$ (More spots than groups):** 
  - Each group automatically promotes its top $k = \lfloor P / G \rfloor$ teams.
  - The remaining $R = P \pmod{G}$ promotion slots are filled by comparing the $(k+1)$-th place teams across all groups using the Global Standing Comparison Formula, promoting the best $R$ teams.

### 3. Selecting Teams for Relegation
Let $R$ be the number of relegation slots for Tier $T$, and $G$ be the number of groups in Tier $T$.
- **Case $R = G$ (Balanced):** The bottom team (10th place) of each of the $G$ groups in Tier $T$ is relegated.
- **Case $R < G$ (Fewer spots than groups):** The 10th place teams from all $G$ groups are compared. The worst $R$ teams (based on the Global Standing Comparison Formula) are relegated.
- **Case $R > G$ (More spots than groups):**
  - Each group automatically relegates its bottom $k = \lfloor R / G \rfloor$ teams.
  - The remaining slots are filled by comparing the $(10-k)$-th place teams across all groups, relegating the worst ones.

### 4. Global Standing Comparison Formula
When comparing teams from different groups within the same tier (to resolve promotion or relegation slots), the following tie-breakers are applied in order to their regular season standings:
1. **Total Points** (Win = 3, Draw = 1, Loss = 0)
2. **Goal Difference** (Total kills scored - Total kills conceded)
3. **Wins** (Total matches won)
4. **Team Type** (Real player `isNpc = false` is prioritized over NPC `isNpc = true`)
5. **Team Reputation** (In-game team attribute)
6. **Team Chemistry** (In-game team attribute)
7. **Team ID** (Lowest ID / ASC - ultimate fallback)

For relegation comparison, the ranking is inverted (lowest points, goal difference, wins, etc. is relegated first; real players are prioritized for safety over NPCs).


### 5. Seeding and Group Assignment for the Next Season
Once the set of teams for each Tier $T$ in the next season is finalized (containing remaining teams + promoted teams + relegated teams), they must be distributed into the groups of Tier $T$:
- To prevent teams from playing the exact same opponents every season, group assignments are shuffled.
- The system gathers all teams assigned to Tier $T$ for the next season.
- The teams are shuffled randomly and then distributed in a round-robin sequence into groups $1 \dots G$ of Tier $T$, ensuring each group receives exactly 10 teams.

## Reward Distribution

At the end of each season (during the **Season Transition** on Week 11, Friday at 19:00), rewards (Gold) are automatically calculated and directly credited to the teams' wallets.

### 1. Reward Configuration Parameters
The rewards system utilizes parameters stored in the `league_tiers_config` or the global game config:
- **Base Tier Rewards:** Each Tier $T$ has a configured `base_gold` (e.g., T1: 5000 Gold, T2: 2500 Gold, T3: 1000 Gold).
- **Season Growth Factor ($g$):** Default is `0.05` (5% compounding increase per season).
- **Promotion Bonus Rate ($B_{promo}$):** Multiplier bonus for teams promoted *into* a higher tier.
  - Promoted into Tier 1 (from T2): +40% bonus ($B_{promo} = 0.40$).
  - Promoted into Tier 2 (from T3): +20% bonus ($B_{promo} = 0.20$).
  - (Promoting to a higher tier grants a progressively larger bonus).
- **Relegation Penalty Rate ($P_{releg}$):** Multiplier penalty for teams relegated *into* a lower tier.
  - Relegated into Tier 2 (from T1): -15% penalty ($P_{releg} = -0.15$).
  - Relegated into Tier 3 (from T2): -10% penalty ($P_{releg} = -0.10$).

### 2. Position Multiplier ($M_{pos}$)
To reward higher placement within a group, the base reward is scaled by the team's final standing position $P \in \{1, \dots, 10\}$:

| Final Position | Multiplier ($M_{pos}$) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st (Group Winner)** | 1.50x | Significant bonus for winning the group. |
| **2nd** | 1.30x | Strong performance reward. |
| **3rd** | 1.15x | Upper-bracket bonus. |
| **4th** | 1.05x | Slight above-average reward. |
| **5th - 6th** | 1.00x | Baseline group reward. |
| **7th - 8th** | 0.90x | Below-average performance penalty. |
| **9th** | 0.80x | Lower-bracket penalty. |
| **10th (Bottom)** | 0.70x | Maximum placement penalty. |

### 3. Compounding Season Growth ($M_{season}$)
To keep rewards competitive as the kingdom ages, the rewards scale with the season number $S$:
$$M_{season}(S) = (1 + g)^{S - 1}$$
*Example (with $g = 0.05$):*
- Season 1: 1.00x
- Season 2: 1.05x
- Season 3: 1.10x
- Season 10: 1.55x

### 4. Promotion/Relegation Modifier ($M_{status}$)
The modifier depends on whether the team promoted, relegated, or remained in the same tier:
- **Promoted:** $M_{status} = 1.0 + B_{promo}(T_{new})$
- **Relegated:** $M_{status} = 1.0 + P_{releg}(T_{new})$
- **Remained in same tier:** $M_{status} = 1.00$

### 5. Final Reward Calculation
For a team finishing at position $P$ in Tier $T_{old}$ during Season $S$, and transitioning to Tier $T_{new}$:
$$\text{Gold Granted} = \text{Base\_Gold}(T_{old}) \times M_{pos}(P) \times M_{season}(S) \times M_{status}$$
*(Final results are rounded to the nearest integer).*

### 6. Distribution, Logging, and History
- **Direct Credit:** Rewards are added directly to the team's balance (`gold` column on the `Team` entity) during the Season Transition transaction. No manual claiming is required.
- **Team chronicle:** `TeamChronicleService::recordSeasonEnded()` writes type `season_ended` to `team_chronicle` (tier, position, promotion/relegation status, gold granted). Displayed on the dashboard and at `/app/chronicle`. See [team-chronicle-system.md](team-chronicle-system.md).
- **Financial Ledger:** A record of type `league_reward` is added to the `FinancialRecord` entity with actor set to `system`, documenting the exact amount of Gold granted for auditing and player ledger transparency.
